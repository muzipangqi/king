Outdoor adventure & cuties animals in Australia

Rottnest Island sits just offshore from the city of Perth, relax on the soft sandy beach and watch the abundance of marine life.
visitors can escape the hustle and bustle of the city and swim in the clear calm waters.

Enjoy these natural wonders during your visit, but at the same time protect this paradise island

  1. Do not feed the wildlife
  2. Do not touch the wildlife
  3. Dispose of your rubbish
  4. Ride your bikes with care

QUOKKA

Rottnest Island is a Class A reserve, known for its high conservation and community values, where the most iconic animals quokkas live.
They are cute and cuddly creatures and are known as the happiest animals in the world, there are between 10,000 and 12,000 live there.

They are mostly nocturnal, but due to the lack of natural predators on the island, they are also active during the day.

Here are the best places to see the quokoos:

  • Settlements around bakeries, beaches, hotels and restaurants
  • Bike parking near Dolphin Cove
  • Under the trees on the hill, walk down Wadjemup Road to the lighthouse
  • Near the Garden Lake
  • It's on Defense Road, just past Digby Road

If you can get a picture with a quokka at the right angle, he looks as though he’s smiling into the camera!
Rottnest Island is highly encouraging of the quokka selfie trend as it helps to promote tourism to the island, which in turn generates funds for vital quokka conservation.

Quokkas are docile, inquisitive, and aren’t afraid of humans, so they don’t appear to be fazed by their social media stardom.

Other areas frequented by the quokkas are around The Basin, Stark Bay headland, Bickley Bay, Parker Point and Salmon Point. The scrub around Bathurst Tennis Courts and Pinky’s Beach are also popular spots.

Prefect view & Adventure

Rottnest Island is surrounded by the expanse of the Indian Ocean, with uncrowded beaches, traffic-free streets, and home to the world-famous Quokkas.
Beaches and bays are all yours to explore, add in snorkelling, surfing, and sunshine; and holidays are made of this.

Just 19 km off the coast of Perth City, but an entire world away, lies the sunny holiday island of Rottnest, blessed with a casual atmosphere, picturesque scenery, dazzling marine life and some of the world's finest beaches and pristine bays.
