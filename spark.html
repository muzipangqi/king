Aboriginal culture tour

The Noongar people are the Aboriginal people of Perth region and it is well worth taking the time to get to know them and where they live.
Kings Park in Perth has been an important place for Aboriginal communities to celebrate and create dreams for thousands of years.

The land we know today as Kings Park has been a place of great significance to its traditional custodians, The land we know today as Kings Park has been a place of great significance to its traditional custodians,
the Whadjuk Noongar people, for at least 40,000 years. The Park's traditional names include 'Kaarta Koomba', 'Kaarta Gar-up' or 'Mooro Kaarta'.

As traditional Noongar songs resonate amongst giant eucalypt and other spectacular native species, it’s easy to imagine the pristine Derbarl Yerrigan (Swan River) winding its way through the virgin landscape pre colonisation. Perth's native flora and city skyline form the beautiful backdrop for this 90 minute walking tour through Karrgatup.
Learn about Kaarta Gar-up, the local Noongar term for the Mt Eliza escarpment in Kings Park, and about Mt Eliza’s intriguing past and its spiritual significance to Noongar people.
The tour begins with a traditional Noongar welcome and ochre ceremony, followed by dreamtime stories and songs featuring Indigenous language.

Governor Weld gazetted a public park of 432 acres here in 1872. With self-government Premier Forrest added a further 450 acres to Mt Eliza Park in 1890 which was renamed Perth Park in 1895.
In 1901 it was renamed Kings Park when Edward VII ascended to the British throne. A few plantings of trees were made including the Avenue of Honour in 1919 for those killed during World War One
In 1929 the Cenotaph was unveiled in Kings Park. The park did not become a Botanic Garden until 1961 when the first director was appointed.The Botanic Gardens officially opened in 1965 with the plantings emphasising WA plants and flowers, including many endemic to this area of Perth.
Kings Park has sandy soils and the 750 mm of annual rainfall soaks away quickly. The Park has a great lookout over the city of Perth and the Swan River and another look out for the Lotterywest Federation Walkway.
In September the Park should have good plantings of several WA wildflowers in garden beds including everlastings, kangaroo paws, verticordia etc.
